Premarriage Course - Homework

Talking your way to peace

This homework prepares us for the next session on communication: listening, timing, honesty, gentle words, and repair when a conversation becomes sensitive.

James 1:19 Be quick to hear, slow to speak, slow to anger.

How to complete this

  1. Pray briefly before you start.
  2. Complete the individual sections separately first.
  3. Then do the couple exercise together.
  4. At the end, copy or download your answers and send them back.

Your answers stay in this browser only. Nothing is uploaded automatically.

1. Who is completing this?

2. Listening map

The book says listening means hearing with thoughtful attention. Answer honestly.

3. The Touchy Map

Both of you have already noticed that "touchy" topics matter. Mark each area green, yellow, or red, then explain one.

4. Shut-off phrases and repair phrases

The book asks whether you use phrases like "I don't want to talk about it" or "I've said all I'm going to say." This section helps you replace shutdown with repair.

5. Words that build

Read Ephesians 4:29 and 1 Thessalonians 5:11. Words should build up, give grace, encourage, and strengthen.

6. Couple exercise - 10 minute listening practice

Do this together after both individual sections are complete. Pick one low-to-medium sensitivity topic. One person speaks for 3 minutes. The other only reflects back: "What I hear you saying is..." Then switch.

7. Optional file note

If you also wrote notes by hand, attach them in the message you send back. You can record the file names here so we know what to expect.
