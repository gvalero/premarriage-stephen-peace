Spiritual Life Together homework

Closer to each other, closer to Jesus

Three jobs in this homework: finish the repair covenant we didn't get to, look honestly at how the wedding decision actually happened, and prepare for our next chapter together — our Spiritual Life Together.

Hebrews 10:24-25 Let us consider how we may spur one another on toward love and good deeds.

Complete in order

  1. Read pages 60-67
  2. Answer separately
  3. Talk together for 20 min

How to complete this

  1. Pray briefly before you start.
  2. Read pages 60-67 of the premarital counselling book: Your Spiritual Life. If you don't have the book to hand, download the chapter as a PDF here (8 pages).
  3. Complete the individual sections separately first.
  4. Then do the 20-minute conversation together (Section 5).
  5. At the end, copy or download your answers and send them back before Monday.

We will go through your answers together on Monday, so short, honest, specific answers are better than long ones. Aim for ~30 min each. Your answers stay in this browser only - nothing is uploaded automatically.

1. Who is completing this?

2. Finishing the Repair Covenant

In Session 3's homework you each named the shape of your shutdown (Stephen: "I need some space"; Peace: "I get quiet when processing my thoughts") and you each drafted a first repair sentence. We didn't get to build the full covenant together in the session - the bit that turns a draft phrase into a promise. So this section is short: just add the two pieces your S3 draft didn't have (how long, when you'll be back), then polish your phrase into the version you'll actually use.

3. The wedding decision - looking at how we got there

Last Monday Stephen opened with the wedding/honeymoon budget story and named it as "probably one of the most significant things for us over the past two months." We didn't fully close that loop.

What this section is NOT: re-opening the decision. You landed somewhere - you're free to keep it. What this section IS: a look at how you made the decision - because that pattern is going to show up again in finances, in roles, in conflict, in everything.

4. The Reading - Your Spiritual Life

Read pp. 60-67 of the book - Your Spiritual Life. If you don't have the book, the chapter is here: download the 8-page PDF.

The chapter is about five things: your personal quiet time, your prayer life, your Bible reading, how you help (or unintentionally hinder) each other spiritually, and how you fit into the body of Christ together. The questions below mirror the ones in the book. Honest answers beat impressive ones - including "I'm not really doing this right now" if that's the truth.

One scripture (optional but encouraged)

The chapter cites many scriptures. Pick ONE that lands and write 2-3 sentences on what it means to you. Suggestions from the chapter: Hebrews 10:24-25, Joshua 1:8, Psalm 1:1-3, Philippians 4:6-7, James 5:16, Philemon 7.

5. Together before Monday - 20 minutes, no phones

After you've both filled in sections 2-4 individually, sit together for 20 minutes, ideally over a meal or coffee.

  1. Compare your repair covenants (§2). Read each other's signal phrase, pause length, return promise, and repair phrase aloud. You don't need to agree on every detail - just hear each other's.
  2. Compare your wedding-decision answers (§3). Specifically: your "1-5 heard score" and your "one thing I'd do differently" answer. Notice - don't argue.
  3. Write one sentence together finishing this prompt:
    "The biggest thing we noticed about HOW we made the wedding decision was ____."

Don't try to resolve it yet - that's what Monday is for.
