Roles in Marriage homework

Roles, repairs, and how we decide

Three jobs in this homework: finish what we didn't get to on shutdown and repair, look honestly at how the wedding decision actually happened, and prepare for our next chapter — Roles in Marriage.

Ephesians 5:21 Submitting to one another in the fear of God.

Complete in order

  1. Read pages 36-53
  2. Answer separately
  3. Talk together for 20 min

How to complete this

  1. Pray briefly before you start.
  2. Read pages 36-53 of the premarital counselling book: The Roles of a Husband and The Roles of a Wife. Read your own section carefully; skim your partner's so you know what's being asked of them.
  3. Complete the individual sections separately first.
  4. Then do the 20-minute conversation together (Section 5).
  5. At the end, copy or download your answers and send them back before Monday.

We will go through your answers together on Monday, so short, honest, specific answers are better than long ones. Aim for ~30 min each. Your answers stay in this browser only - nothing is uploaded automatically.

1. Who is completing this?

2. The Repair Covenant

Last Sunday we named the shape of your shutdown. Stephen wrote: "I need some space." Peace wrote: "I get quiet when processing my thoughts." Both healthy in shape, costly when the other person is left guessing. We didn't get to build a repair covenant in the session - let's draft it now, individually first. We'll lock it in together on Monday.

3. The wedding decision - looking at how we got there

Last Sunday Stephen opened with the wedding/honeymoon budget story and named it as "probably one of the most significant things for us over the past two months." We didn't fully close that loop.

What this section is NOT: re-opening the decision. You landed somewhere - you're free to keep it. What this section IS: a look at how you made the decision - because that pattern is going to show up again in finances, in roles, in conflict, in everything.

4. The Roles Reading

Read pp. 36-53 - The Roles of a Husband (pp. 36-44) and The Roles of a Wife (pp. 45-53). Read your own section carefully; skim your partner's section so you understand what's being asked of them.

A note before you read: this chapter is direct. It uses words like headship, submission, helper, provider. Don't let the vocabulary trigger you before you've read what the book actually means by them. The book opens with: "God created man and woman equal in His sight. We are not inferior or superior to each other." Read with curiosity, not defence. Bring your honest reactions on Monday.

For Stephen - pp. 36-44

The chapter frames the husband's role in four parts: headship, provider, protector, lover.

For Peace - pp. 45-53

The chapter frames the wife's role around two words people often misread: submit and respect. Read the book's framing carefully before reacting. The verse the chapter is built on is Eph 5:21-22, and verse 21 (right before the famous one) says: "submitting to one another in the fear of God." That mutual context matters.

5. Together before Monday - 20 minutes, no phones

After you've both filled in sections 2-4 individually, sit together for 20 minutes, ideally over a meal or coffee.

  1. Compare your repair covenants (§2). Read each other's signal phrase, pause length, return promise, and repair phrase aloud. You don't need to agree on every detail - just hear each other's.
  2. Compare your wedding-decision answers (§3). Specifically: your "1-5 heard score" and your "one thing I'd do differently" answer. Notice - don't argue.
  3. Write one sentence together finishing this prompt:
    "The biggest thing we noticed about HOW we made the wedding decision was ____."

Don't try to resolve it yet - that's what Monday is for.
